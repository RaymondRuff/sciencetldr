## Top Peer-Reviewed Papers (ranked by DICE score)

### 1. Cibisatamab and FAP-4-1BBL in microsatellite-stable colorectal cancer: a phase 1b trial

Melero I, Tanos T, Calvo Aller E, et al.

*Nature Medicine*

[10.1038/s41591-026-04380-z](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41591-026-04380-z)

CEA × CD3 IgG-based TCE (cibisatamab) combined with FAP-targeted 4-1BBL trimer for tumor-localized co-stimulation; obinutuzumab pretreatment to mitigate ADAs.

First clinical readout pairing a CEA × CD3 engager with a tumor-anchored 4-1BB agonist in MSS mCRC, a setting where TCE monotherapy historically fails. Confirmed PRs in 7/52 patients with paired biopsies showing increased intratumoral CD8+/Ki67+ T cells, validating the "engage + costim" architecture as a translatable engineering paradigm beyond bispecific format alone.

DICE score: **4** — Clinical proof-of-concept that tumor-localized 4-1BB co-stimulation can be safely layered onto a CEAxCD3 TCE in cold solid tumors, even though monotherapy responses remain modest.

Clinical-stage. Originator: Roche / Genentech (Pablo Umaña group, Roche Innovation Center Zurich).

### 2. Targeted TNF Potentiates the Activity of Bispecific T-cell Engagers in Solid Tumors by Turning Cold Tumors Hot

Thorhallsdottir G, Benz R, Faviana P, et al.

*Cancer Immunology Research*

[10.1158/2326-6066.CIR-25-1572](https://doi.org/10.1158/2326-6066.CIR-25-1572)

L19-TNF (anti-EDB fibronectin TNF fusion) + CEA × CD3 TCE; vascular-modulating immunocytokine paired with TCE.

Mechanistic and in vivo demonstration that vascular-localized TNF delivery upregulates ICAM-1, increases TCE extravasation, and converts immune-excluded CRC tumors into inflamed lesions, yielding complete regression and rechallenge protection in mouse models. Provides a biology-driven engineering rationale for combining TCEs with tumor-targeted cytokine fusions rather than systemic checkpoint blockade.

DICE score: **4** — A differentiated mechanism for rescuing TCE activity in cold solid tumors with strong preclinical evidence and a clear translational path (Philogen's L19-TNF is already clinical-stage).

Originating lab: Dario Neri / Philochem-Philogen.

### 3. ABL209 (NEOK002): Designing an EGFR×MUC1 bispecific TOP1i ADC with promising anti-tumor activity and enhanced therapeutic window

Lee B, Park HJ, Yoo BM, et al.

*Molecular Cancer Therapeutics*

[10.1158/1535-7163.MCT-26-0088](https://doi.org/10.1158/1535-7163.MCT-26-0088)

EGFR × MUC1 heterodimeric 1+1 bispecific ADC (DAR 4, tavatecan TOP1 inhibitor payload).

Couples a bispecific antibody scaffold with a TOP1i payload to break the EGFR skin-toxicity dose ceiling: dual binding drives selective internalization in EGFR+/MUC1+ tumors while sparing keratinocytes, with regressions in 78% of 36 PDX models including KRAS-mutant tumors. A clean engineering case study of how avidity-based dual antigen targeting can widen the therapeutic window of a high-potency payload.

DICE score: **4** — Differentiated bispecific-ADC format with strong PDX dataset and a credible toxicity-mitigation mechanism, advancing the increasingly crowded EGFR-ADC landscape.

Originator: ABL Bio (clinical-stage pipeline).

### 4. Pharmacologic glycoengineering of FcγRIIIa enhances force-resistant IgG-Fc binding and anti-tumor antibody efficacy

[Authors per PubMed record]

*Immunity*

[https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42034063/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42034063/)

Glycan engineering of FcγRIIIa (effector-cell side, not the antibody) to stabilize IgG-Fc receptor catch bonds under shear.

Inverts the usual antibody-side glycoengineering paradigm by tuning the receptor's glycan to enhance force-resistant IgG-FcγR contacts, yielding measurably stronger ADCC and tumor control. Suggests a new lever for engager and Fc-competent multispecific design — pairing molecules with cell-state-dependent FcγR glyco-context.

DICE score: **4** — Novel mechanistic insight in *Immunity* opening an under-explored axis of effector engineering relevant to Fc-bearing multispecifics.

### 5. Mosunetuzumab plus polatuzumab vedotin in relapsed/refractory MCL after BTK inhibitor therapy: a phase 2 study

Budde LE, Kamdar M, Assouline SE, et al.

*Blood*

[10.1182/blood.2025032422](https://doi.org/10.1182/blood.2025032422)

CD20 × CD3 bispecific (mosunetuzumab) + CD79b ADC (polatuzumab vedotin) in MCL post BTKi.

First reported bispecific-ADC combination in MCL: ORR 88.1% (CR 78.6%) in 42 high-risk patients including 26% with prior CAR-T; CRS limited to G1-2. Establishes a fixed-duration outpatient regimen and supports the broader thesis of pairing TCEs with non-overlapping ADC mechanisms for orthogonal tumor killing.

DICE score: **3** — Important clinical validation of bispecific + ADC layered cytotoxicity in a difficult MCL population.

Clinical-stage. Roche/Genentech.

### 6. Multicenter Real-World Analysis of Glofitamab in Relapsed/Refractory Primary CNS Lymphoma: Clinical Activity, CNS Penetration, and ctDNA Dynamics

[Authors per PubMed record]

*American Journal of Hematology*

[https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42035265/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42035265/)

CD20 × CD3 IgG-like 2:1 TCE (glofitamab) in PCNSL.

Real-world dataset documenting CSF penetration and clinical activity of a 2:1 CD20 × CD3 engager in primary CNS lymphoma — an unusual sanctuary site for Fc-containing engagers. CNS exposure plus ctDNA pharmacodynamics inform target-site design considerations for future engagers (size, FcRn engagement, brain-blood partitioning).

DICE score: **3** — Useful translational data for a high-unmet-need site that may shape future engager format choices.

Clinical-stage. Roche.

### 9. Modeling chimeric antigen receptor response at the single-cell level with conditional optimal transport

Driessen A, Born J, Castellanos Rueda R, Reddy ST, Rapsomaniki M.

*Cell Systems*

[10.1016/j.cels.2026.101591](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cels.2026.101591)

Conditional optimal-transport framework over protein-language-model CAR embeddings to predict single-cell responses including out-of-distribution variants.

A generalizable design-function model that maps CAR sequence variants onto single-cell transcriptional outcomes and extrapolates to unseen CARs. Conceptually transferable to engager design — bridging sequence-level engineering decisions with downstream functional response is a recurring bottleneck for binding-domain selection and architecture choice.

DICE score: **3** — Methodologically interesting; signals where engager design tooling is heading (PLM embeddings + single-cell function modeling). Sai Reddy / IBM Research collaboration.

### 10. Efficiency landscape of bioorthogonal click reactions producing bispecific antibody conjugates

Petri L, Kovács ÍK, Szabó B, et al. (incl. Chudasama V, Szijj PA, Keserű GM)

*Cell Reports Methods*

[10.1016/j.crmeth.2026.101416](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.crmeth.2026.101416)

### 11. First-in-human study of intravenous bispecific CTLA-4 × OX40 antibody (ATOR-1015) in advanced solid malignancies

Ekström-Rydén V, Carneiro A, Yachnin J, et al.

*ESMO Open*

[10.1016/j.esmoop.2026.106068](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.esmoop.2026.106068)

Tumor-conditional CTLA-4 × OX40 bispecific IgG (Alligator Bioscience).

Phase I across 28 patients showed safety and pharmacodynamic immune activation but heavy neutralizing-ADA formation that capped exposure and efficacy. The authors explicitly call for re-engineering of the format to reduce immunogenicity — a useful, public, candid case study of how bispecific format choice flows into the de-immunization workstream.

DICE score: **3** — Clinical-stage data with a clear engineering lesson on immunogenicity for combined immune-modulator bispecifics.

Clinical-stage. Originator: Alligator Bioscience.

## Weekly Themes

This week's literature underscores two converging trends in multispecific engineering. First, **TCE potency in cold solid tumors increasingly depends on co-delivered tumor-microenvironment modulation** — both Roche's clinical FAP-4-1BBL/cibisatamab combo (Nat Med) and Philogen's preclinical L19-TNF/CEAxCD3 combo (Cancer Immunol Res) argue that single-molecule engager engineering has reached diminishing returns and that companion TME-conditioning agents are now part of the design problem. Kuhlman's de novo TNFL masking domains (bioRxiv) point to where this is heading: programmable, conditionally active cytokine arms built into the engager itself. Second, **computational and energetics-driven binder design is maturing into routine tooling**, with repertoire-scale structural prediction (Sci Adv), AF-Multimer GPCR nanobody discovery (Nat Comm), optimal-transport CAR-response modeling (Cell Systems), and TCR energetic mapping (bioRxiv) all appearing in a single week. Bispecific-ADC layering (Mosun-Pola in MCL; ABL209 EGFRxMUC1) and renewed scrutiny of Fc-silencing assumptions (Georgiou, FCRL5) round out a notably engineering-heavy week.
