## Top Papers

### 1. A trophoblast glycoprotein specific 5T4-Vδ2 bispecific T cell engager recruits Vγ9Vδ2-T cells for tumor-selective cytotoxicity across solid malignancies

Authors: de Jong, Žiberna, Veth, Michielon, Myrda, Seidel, Akbarzadeh, van Helden, Vis, Derks, Lameris, de Gruijl, van der Vliet

Journal: Clinical Immunology

DOI/link: [10.1016/j.clim.2026.110707](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clim.2026.110707)

Target / format: 5T4 × Vδ2 TCR, γδT-cell engager (scFv-VHH tandem).

Summary: Novel non-αβ engager that redirects innate-like Vγ9Vδ2 T cells toward the oncofetal antigen 5T4, sidestepping the MHC-restriction, tonic activation, and healthy-tissue toxicity issues of CD3-based TCEs in solid tumors. The paper benchmarks potency and tumor-selective killing across a panel of epithelial malignancies and supports γδ-engager formats as a differentiated solid-tumor modality.

DICE score: 4 — Differentiated engager architecture (γδ-TCR arm) with demonstrated advantages for solid tumors, a key unmet need for the field.

Flag: Preclinical. Group: de Gruijl / van der Vliet (Amsterdam UMC), a prominent γδ-engager lab (LAVA-1207 lineage).

### 2. Preclinical characterization of AZD1163, a first-in-class anti-PAD2/4 bispecific antibody for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis

Authors: Sims, Scaletta, Andrews, Strain, Sigurdardottir, Kuriakose, England, Rajasinghe, Jiang, Xiong, Neal, Vinall, Newton, Boger, Kim, Dahlbäck, Prothon, Leander, Darrah, Collins, Douglas, White, et al.

Journal: MAbs

DOI/link: [10.1080/19420862.2026.2657629](https://doi.org/10.1080/19420862.2026.2657629)

Target / format: PAD2 × PAD4 bispecific IgG.

Summary: First-in-class dual inhibitor of peptidylarginine deiminase 2 and 4 engineered as a single IgG, designed to shut down citrullinated-autoantigen generation upstream of the ACPA response in rheumatoid arthritis. Paper reports discovery, epitope mapping against the two enzyme isoforms, enzymatic blockade, and PK/efficacy in disease models, anchoring an IND-enabling package.

DICE score: 4 — A clinically-headed, first-in-class enzymatic dual-blocker bispecific for autoimmune disease, showing bsAb utility beyond receptor crosslinking.

Flag: Clinical-stage candidate. Group: AstraZeneca BioPharmaceuticals (R&D).

### 3. A Bispecific Antibody Engineering Strategy for Effective Dual-Target Degradation of Cell-Surface and Secreted Oncoproteins with Superior Antitumor Activity

Authors: Zhang, Ma, Zheng, Fang, Sun, Li, Cai

Journal: ACS Chemical Biology

DOI/link: [10.1021/acschembio.6c00123](https://doi.org/10.1021/acschembio.6c00123)

Target / format: HER2 × secreted ligand, bispecific IgG1 "Bi-SignalTAC" (lysosome-targeting degrader).

Summary: Extends LYTAC-style degradation into a bispecific IgG that simultaneously drags a cell-surface oncodriver (HER2) and its tumor-microenvironment protein ligand to the lysosome via the intrinsic sorting machinery. Demonstrates that a single antibody scaffold can function as a dual-cargo degrader, broadening the design space for degradation-based biologics beyond PROTAC-like and single-target LYTAC formats.

DICE score: 4 — Introduces a useful new bispecific modality (dual-substrate degrader) with clear demonstrated antitumor advantages over parental mAbs.

Flag: Preclinical.

### 4. DualGPT-AB: a dual-stage generative optimization framework for therapeutic antibody design

Authors: (Nature Computational Science)

Journal: Nature Computational Science

DOI/link: [10.1038/s43588-026-00976-0](https://doi.org/10.1038/s43588-026-00976-0)

Target / format: Platform (computational) — dual-stage conditional GPT for VH/VL co-optimization.

Summary: Generative transformer framework that simultaneously optimizes binding specificity, viscosity, clearance, and immunogenicity during antibody sequence design, arguing that decoupling target-conditioned generation from developability refinement yields better multi-objective trade-offs than single-stage models. Directly applicable to the V-regions that populate bispecific/TCE binding arms and to manufacturability-constrained engager pipelines.

DICE score: 4 — Notable multi-property generative platform with direct transferability to engager arm design.

Flag: Computational (no clinical flag).

### 5. Artificial allosteric protein switches with machine-learning-designed receptors

Authors: (Nature Biotechnology)

Journal: Nature Biotechnology

DOI/link: [10.1038/s41587-026-03081-9](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-026-03081-9)

Target / format: De novo ligand-binding domains used as single-component allosteric switch receptors.

Summary: Uses ML-engineered minimal ligand-binding domains as plug-and-play receptors for single-chain allosteric switches, reporting that efficient switching does not require large global conformational change. Establishes a route toward conditionally-active binders — highly relevant for designing logic-gated or tumor-microenvironment-responsive engager arms that limit on-target/off-tumor toxicity.

DICE score: 4 — Transformative enabling platform for conditional biologics; applicable to next-generation engager safety switches.

### 6. Taking the biology seriously makes models better (biologically-informed protein language models for antibody affinity maturation)

Authors: (eLife insight; primary work associated)

Journal: eLife

DOI/link: [10.7554/eLife.111070](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.111070)

Target / format: Protein language model training paradigm for antibody variable regions.

Summary: A biologically-informed training paradigm conditions protein language models on the B-cell maturation process so they predict affinity maturation trajectories rather than merely fitting static sequence statistics. DICE score: 4 — Shifts antibody PLM design toward explicitly modeling maturation dynamics; immediately useful for engager arm affinity maturation.

DOI/link: [10.1111/cas.70376](https://doi.org/10.1111/cas.70376)

Authors: Li, McGee, Wong, Grinstaff

Server: bioRxiv

DOI/link: [10.64898/2026.04.18.719371](https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.04.18.719371)

Target / format: CD19 × CD3 scFv-scFv BiTE expressed from a modified self-amplifying RNA (saRNA) delivered by LNP.

Summary: Demonstrates that a single LNP-saRNA dose sustains in vivo expression of a CD19 BiTE at therapeutic levels long enough to clear malignant B cells in mouse models, potentially replacing continuous IV infusion of short-half-life BiTE protein with a one-shot nucleic-acid dose. ## Weekly Themes

Two strong through-lines this week. First, solid-tumor T cell engagers are getting serious protein-engineering attention — the Cancer Cell review frames the problem space, and the γδ-TCR engager (5T4×Vδ2) and tumor-tethered CD40 bispecific (KK2269) are concrete attempts to fix the therapeutic-window problem by switching either the effector arm or the activation logic. Second, computational antibody design continues to mature in the direction multispecifics actually need: DualGPT-AB multi-property generation, ML-designed conditional allosteric receptors, biologically-informed PLMs for affinity maturation, and LinkCraft for inter-domain geometry together sketch a pipeline where generative design, developability, and linker biophysics are co-optimized rather than handled in sequence. The week's highest-impact item is a preprint — a single-dose LNP-saRNA CD19 BiTE — which, if it generalizes, would retire one of the defining manufacturing/PK limitations of the BiTE class.
