## Top Papers

### 1. Engineering of acidic pH-responsive anti-CD3 binding antibodies

La Sala G, Kroell KB, Pincha M, Gassner C, Deho L, Moessner E, et al.

*mAbs*

[10.1080/19420862.2026.2658902](https://doi.org/10.1080/19420862.2026.2658902)

CD3ε binders engineered for pH-conditional T cell activation in the acidic tumor microenvironment.

Roche pRED engineers conditional pH-dependent CD3 binders that preferentially engage T cells at the acidic tumor pH (~6.5) but not at physiological pH (7.4), starting from the 40G5c CD3ε scaffold and using molecular-dynamics simulations to rationalize and guide pH-switching mutations at the VH–VL interface. The resulting variants retain affinity, show clean pH-window profiles, and selectively activate T cells at acidic pH — providing a TCE engineering route that decouples on-target efficacy from off-tumor T cell activation without relying solely on tumor-antigen specificity.

DICE score: 4 — Differentiated, mechanism-grounded engineering route to widen the TCE therapeutic window by exploiting tumor pH, with MD-validated structural rationale for the key mutations.

Originating lab: Christian Klein / Anna Vangone, Roche pRED Zurich/Munich (Prescient Design).

### 2. De novo engineered disulfide bond supersedes native interchain linkage to enhance TCR pairing and anti-tumor efficacy in T cell therapy

Su D, Zhu F, Shi H, Yuan Y, Wang J, Yan X, et al.

*Cellular & Molecular Immunology*

[10.1038/s41423-026-01418-7](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41423-026-01418-7)

TCR α/β constant-domain engineering with computationally placed de novo disulfides (DSS-TCR).

Structure-guided computational placement of nine new α/β interchain disulfide pairs, combined with α-chain transmembrane hydrophobic substitutions, produces TCR-T architectures that surface-express better than wild-type and match murinized constant-region performance without the immunogenicity penalty of mouse sequences. The platform extends to other TCR-based receptors, providing a portable solution to chain mispairing that is directly relevant to engineering TCR-mimic engagers and pMHC-targeted bispecifics where exogenous–endogenous mispairing is the dominant safety/efficacy bottleneck.

DICE score: 4 — Generalizable, computationally driven solution to a long-standing TCR mispairing problem with implications well beyond TCR-T into engager/scaffold design.

Originating lab: Guangna Liu / Wei Rui, Hunan University and TSAR Therapeutics.

### 3. A Tri-specific EGFR/cMet/VEGF Antibody Demonstrates Potent Multi-Mechanistic Activity in Preclinical Triple-Negative Breast Cancer Models

Pu P, Jin Y, Zhang S, Xu Y, Chen P, Fulai Z, et al.

*Journal of Biological Chemistry*

[10.1016/j.jbc.2026.113100](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jbc.2026.113100)

Trispecific EGFR (dual-epitope) × cMet (dual-epitope) × VEGF antibody (TAVO412) with retained Fc effector function.

TAVO412 combines biparatopic anti-EGFR and anti-cMet arms with a VEGF-neutralizing arm in a single trispecific scaffold. The dual-epitope avidity gives it superior signaling inhibition and ADCC against EGFR-low TNBC cells than the marketed amivantamab (JNJ-61186372) bispecific benchmark, with anti-angiogenic activity layered on top. The result is a useful demonstration that biparatopic avidity, not just adding a third specificity, is the dominant driver of differentiated activity in higher-order multispecifics.

DICE score: 3 — Concrete head-to-head preclinical evidence that biparatopic avidity in a trispecific outperforms an approved bispecific in low-antigen settings.

Originator: Tavotek Biotherapeutics.

### 4. T cell-engaging bispecific antibodies for myeloid malignancies: Targets, formats, and clinical challenges

Paczesny S, Barwe SP, Gopalakrishnapillai A, Cheung NV.

*Cell Reports Medicine*

[10.1016/j.xcrm.2026.102772](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.xcrm.2026.102772)

Review of T-BsAb formats for AML and related myeloid disease (CD33, CD123, CLL-1, FLT3 × CD3 axes).

Synthesizes how engineering choices — affinity-attenuated CD3 arms, half-life and avidity tuning, masked / conditionally-active formats, and combination logic — are converging to address the core problem of hitting AML blasts without ablating normal hematopoiesis. Most useful as a structured map of which formats cluster around which targets and where the field's engineering bottlenecks (myelotoxicity, target heterogeneity, T cell dysfunction in AML BM) actually sit.

DICE score: 3 — Authoritative synthesis from a leading TCE chemist (Cheung, MSK) on the hardest engager problem space, with concrete engineering takeaways.

### 5. Evaluation of Tissue from Patients with Prostate Cancer Identifies B7-H3 as an Androgen-Regulated, Broadly-Expressed, Combinatorial Therapeutic Target

Sharma S, Mundhara N, Tekoglu E, Amaral A, Gu P, Luo J, et al.

*Clinical Cancer Research*

[10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-26-0642](https://doi.org/10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-26-0642)

Single-cell-atlas-driven framework for nominating bispecific antigen pairs in prostate cancer (B7-H3 × TROP2 / NECTIN1 / KLK2 / NECTIN4).

Integrates a ~1M-cell, 213-patient prostate atlas (JHU-PANORAMA) with PDX surfaceome data to score combinatorial bispecific targets across hormone-sensitive, CRPC, neuroendocrine, and "double-negative" disease states. Identifies B7-H3 as the only broadly and uniformly expressed surface antigen across the disease continuum and proposes a systematic, AR-context-aware framework for engager target-pair selection. The reusable framework is the main engineering contribution; B7-H3 × TROP2 / NECTIN1 emerge as the highest-scoring combinatorial bispecific candidates.

DICE score: 3 — Useful, reusable methodology that extends single-cell atlases past biomarker discovery into operational bispecific antigen-pair design.

### 6. Targeting the immunological synapse in multiple myeloma

Sumei L, Bahmani F.

*Discover Oncology*

[10.1007/s12672-026-05125-7](https://doi.org/10.1007/s12672-026-05125-7)

Mechanistic review on BiTE vs CAR-T synapse architecture in MM and forward look at trispecifics, armored / logic-gated CAR-T, niche-modifying agents.

Frames TCE and CAR-T progress through the lens of synapse fidelity in the bone-marrow niche, contrasting how BiTEs assemble cytolytic synapses versus the reorganized synaptic interfaces of CAR-T cells. The most useful section is the engineering-forward discussion linking trispecific costim-arm design and logic gating to synapse resilience under MM-niche–driven T cell dysfunction — a direct hypothesis source for next-gen format choices in BCMA / GPRC5D engagers.

DICE score: 3 — Mechanism-focused review that translates synapse biology into concrete next-gen engager engineering hypotheses.

### 7. Real-world incidence of severe infections in multiple myeloma patients receiving bispecific antibodies: a meta-analysis

Spataro F, Desantis V, Einsele H, Dammacco F, Vacca A, Ria R, Solimando AG.

*Annals of Hematology*

[10.1007/s00277-026-07026-9](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00277-026-07026-9)

Meta-analysis of grade 3-4 infection rates with teclistamab (CD3 × BCMA) and talquetamab (CD3 × GPRC5D).

Pooled grade 3-4 infection rate of 25% across 1,373 RRMM patients on approved BiTEs, with comparable rates between BCMA and GPRC5D engagers. Meta-regression suggests fitter, less heavily pretreated patients preferentially receive these molecules in real-world practice — meaning trial CRS / infection profiles may understate the toxicity ceiling once the molecules move earlier in treatment lines and into combinations.

DICE score: 3 — Quantifies a critical engager-class liability with real-world data and direct implications for engineering of less-immunosuppressive next-gen formats (sub-Q dosing, attenuated CD3 arms, BCMA-sparing scaffolds).

Clinical-stage. Multi-center European consortium led by University of Bari.

### 8. Elranatamab: Mechanism of Action, Clinical, and Translational Science

Elmeliegy M, Viqueira A, Vandendries E, Soltantabar P, Lon HK, Poels K, et al.

*Clinical and Translational Science*

[10.1111/cts.70554](https://doi.org/10.1111/cts.70554)

CD3 × BCMA IgG-format bispecific (elranatamab) — translational PK/PD, step-up dosing, MagnetisMM data integration.

Pfizer's translational-science synthesis ties the 12 → 32 → 76 mg subcutaneous step-up regimen and tapered Q1W → Q2W → Q4W dosing to PK/PD modeling and CRS mitigation across MagnetisMM-1/2/3/9. A useful reference example of how dose-architecture engineering — not just molecule engineering — defines the deliverable therapeutic index of a CD3-redirecting BiTE.

DICE score: 2 — Useful translational/regulatory benchmark for CD3 × BCMA engagers; consolidates known data rather than introducing new advances.

Clinical-stage. Pfizer.

### 9. Blinatumomab in pediatric acute lymphoblastic leukemia: current and future use

Lertvivatpong N, Inaba H.

*Leukemia*

[10.1038/s41375-026-02962-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41375-026-02962-x)

CD19 × CD3 BiTE (blinatumomab); review of frontline and r/r pediatric B-ALL with subcutaneous formulation prospects.

Synthesizes recent randomized data showing that blinatumomab improves outcomes when added to or substituting chemotherapy in pediatric B-ALL, and discusses the failure modes — CD19 loss / downregulation, T cell dysfunction, CNS limits, short half-life — that motivate ongoing format engineering (subcutaneous formulation, longer-half-life variants, combinations with checkpoint blockade or CD22 redirection).

DICE score: 2 — Useful state-of-field synthesis for the prototypical BiTE; few new engineering insights.

## Notable Preprints

### 1. A Chimeric Receptor Enabling Antibody-Guided Retargeting of CAR T-Cells

Vinanica N, Yeo J, Hamran NAN, Campana D.

*bioRxiv* (preprint)

[10.64898/2026.04.27.720976](https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.04.27.720976)

CARFcR — CAR with an embedded CD16V158 Fc receptor enabling dual CAR + antibody-driven targeting; demonstrated for CD19, BCMA, CD123, CD33.

A modular architecture that fuses the cytotoxicity of conventional CAR-T cells with the targeting plasticity of clinical antibodies (trastuzumab, rituximab) by inserting a CD16V158 Fc receptor into the CAR extracellular domain. Importantly, CARFcR-T cells are also redirectable by CD16-engaging bispecific engagers, blurring the line between cell therapy and engager design and offering a route to overcome antigen escape (anti-CD19 CARFcR-T cells eliminate CD19-negative escape variants when guided by trastuzumab or rituximab).

DICE score: 4 — Genuinely platform-level: same CAR backbone repurposable across antigens by changing the antibody payload, with explicit synergy with CD16-binding engagers.

Originating lab: Dario Campana, National University of Singapore.

### 2. Allogeneic CRISPR-Engineered CAR-T Cells Drive Potent Antitumor Activity in Solid Tumors

Huo M, Li D, Li N, Quan A, Liang T, Henderson D, et al.

*bioRxiv* (preprint)

[10.64898/2026.04.25.720815](https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.04.25.720815)

Off-the-shelf TRAC-knock-in / B2M-knockout CAR-T against GPC2 (neuroblastoma) and GPC3 (HCC) using AAV donor + CRISPR-Cas9.

CRISPR-Cas9 + AAV-donor delivery places a CAR cassette at the TRAC locus in healthy-donor T cells with concurrent B2M knockout. Repeat-dose efficacy in GPC2 and GPC3 solid-tumor xenografts without escalating toxicity supports allogeneic multi-dosing for solid tumors — the same problem domain that next-gen engagers are trying to crack, and a useful preclinical readout on glypican-family targeting more broadly.

DICE score: 3 — Useful preclinical proof that GPC-family allogeneic CAR-T can be redosed; informs target-selection logic for engagers in low-prevalence pediatric and adult solid tumors.

Originating lab: Mitchell Ho, NCI / NIH.

### 3. Generative design of sequence-specific DNA-binding proteins

Sehgal E, Politanska Y, Mitra R, Kim PT, Gonzalez Rodriguez N, Warrier T, et al.

*bioRxiv* (preprint)

[10.64898/2026.04.27.720408](https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.04.27.720408)

Deep-learning–based de novo design of sequence-specific DNA-binding proteins.

Baker-lab pipeline that designs DNA-sequence-specific binders from scratch using a generative diffusion approach. Not directly an engager paper, but the same generative-design backbone is the underlying tooling now being used to design de novo binders against protein tumor antigens for engager arms — worth tracking as the precursor tech for next-gen multispecific scaffolds where each arm is fully engineered rather than discovered by panning.

DICE score: 3 — Field-tracking value: the same generative pipelines are the precursor technology for de novo engager-arm design.

Originating lab: David Baker, Institute for Protein Design / University of Washington.

## Weekly Themes

This week's most engager-relevant signal is the convergence of conditional-activation engineering with quantitative, structure-guided computation: Roche's MD-guided pH-responsive CD3 binders and the Hunan / TSAR de novo TCR disulfide work both lean on simulation rather than empirical screening to widen the therapeutic window or fix a long-standing pairing problem. On the architecture side, higher-order multispecifics (TAVO412 trispecific) and CAR–antibody hybrids (CARFcR) are pushing past two-target designs toward avidity- and modularity-driven differentiation, with explicit cross-talk to the engager modality. Two clinical-translational signals — meta-analytic infection rates with BCMA / GPRC5D BiTEs and the Pfizer elranatamab translational-science paper — reinforce that next-generation engager design must optimize for hypogammaglobulinemia and CRS as much as cytotoxic potency, particularly as these molecules move earlier in treatment lines.
